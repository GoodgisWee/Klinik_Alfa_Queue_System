Chime Sound Options

Click each button to play that option (you can click as many times as you like). Tell me the number/name of the one you want to use on the display screen, or describe a tweak.

1. Current (soft, for reference)

What's live now - ding=C6, dong=G5, lower volume.

2. Louder & Higher Ding-Dong

Same melody shape, shifted up to G6/E6 and noticeably louder.

3. Very Bright Ding-Dong

Even higher (C7/G6), loud - the brightest/most piercing of the ding-dong style.

4. Triple Ascending Chime

Three quick rising notes (C6-E6-G6) instead of two - common in PA/intercom systems.

5. Sharp Alert (triangle wave)

Same high two-tone melody but a sharper, more "electronic alert" timbre.

6. Loud Buzzer Beep

Harsher double-beep (square wave) - most attention-grabbing, less "pleasant".

More Ding-Dong Variations

All two-note "ding-dong" style, tuned for clarity and cutting through ambient noise.

7. Classic Doorbell

The "ding-dong" interval most people instantly recognize (E6 down to C6), loud.

8. Rich Harmonic Bell

Adds a third harmonic layer (octave + fifth) for a fuller, more defined "bell" clarity.

9. Pure Clean Tone

No overtone at all - just one strong, very loud sine per note. Less "musical", maximally clear/punchy.

10. Echo Ding-Dong

Normal ding-dong plus a softer echo repeat right after, for a fuller "PA system" presence.

11. Ding-Ding-Dong

Two quick "dings" then a "dong" - the 3-hit pattern real pharmacy/bank queue systems often use.

12. Big Bold Ding-Dong

Highest pitch, loudest, fastest punchy attack - the most attention-grabbing two-tone option.
